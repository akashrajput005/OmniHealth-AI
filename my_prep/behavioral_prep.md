## 🎬 Situational Questions (STAR Method)

### Q1: "Tell me about a time you had to learn a new technology quickly."
- **Situation**: For LiverSegNet, I needed to implement real-time surgical tool tracking.
- **Task**: I hadn't worked with **DeepLabV3+ and ASPP** modules in a clinical context before.
- **Action**: I audited several medical CV research papers and implemented a **Dual-Kernel** approach to separate organ and tool perception.
- **Result**: Achieved <50ms latency—meeting the requirements for real-time surgical support.

### Q2: "How do you handle disagreement in a team?"
- **Situation**: During a project, there was a debate on whether to use a single massive transformer or a hybrid model.
- **Task**: I advocated for the hybrid approach to ensure safety.
- **Action**: I created a **Master Technical Report** and an **Audit Frame** (like `audit_frame_99.py`) to show the failure cases of "Pure AI" in shadows vs. the hybrid success.
- **Result**: The team was convinced by the data, and we built a more resilient system.

## ❓ Questions for the Interviewer (Demonstrate Depth)

1.  **Technical**: "How does your team ensure the **temporal stability** of models when they are deployed on real-time streaming data vs. static test sets?"
2.  **Product**: "Do you see [Company Product/Service] moving towards more **Explainable AI (XAI)** in the next year?"
3.  **Culture**: "What’s the most interesting 'Edge Case' the data science team solved recently?"
4.  **Growth**: "What are the key traits of an entry-level engineer who succeeds in your team during their first 6 months?"
